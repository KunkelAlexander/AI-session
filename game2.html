    • It picks likely words, not correct ones — that's why it can sound confident and still be wrong.
    • It doesn't know where any word came from, so don't trust it to cite its sources.

    ✍️ I wrote like an AI: one likely word at a time. This is similar to how a large language model generates text. At each step, it sees the entire context generated so far, but it doesn't know exactly how the sentence will end. Small differences in early word choices can lead to very different outcomes, and maintaining a long, coherent chain of reasoning can be challenging because each new word is chosen incrementally rather than from a fully planned argument.
